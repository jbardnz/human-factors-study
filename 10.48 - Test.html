SHORT TEST: SHORT TEST 10.48

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.48
Question 1:

Most aircraft accidents result from a combination of circumstances rather than a single cause. In reality, accidents are the result of a chain of causes, a series of errors in judgement. This is commonly known as?

The good judgement behavior chain or GJ chain
The poor judgement behavior chain or PJ chain
Get home-itis
The broken chain effect
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: The poor judgement behavior chain or PJ chain - 10.48.6

Question 2:

Too much pride has resulted in many aircraft accidents merely because the pilot didn’t want to have the shame of turning back in deteriorating weather or admit that they were extending themselves beyond their personal limits. This type of poor decision-making is commonly known as?

Machismo
Anti Authority
Good judgement
Get-home-itis
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Get-home-itis - 10.48.20

Question 3:

Good judgement in pilots:

Is something a good pilot is usually born with
Is not an important aspect of flight safety
Cannot be improved with training courses
Will help to prevent pilots from taking unnecessary risks
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Will help to prevent pilots from taking unnecessary risks - 10.48.2

Question 4:

Select the incorrect statement below:

Most aircraft accidents result from a combination of circumstances rather than a single cause
Judgements are based on information the pilot has about themself, the aircraft and the environment, and a pilot is less likely to make a poor judgement if this information is accurate
Since pilot judgement is a mental process, pilots can be trained (or retrained) to use good judgement in the first place, or to stop the influence of poor judgement
Most aircraft accidents result from a single cause
Breaking the Poor Judgement chain is an act of good pilot judgement
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Most aircraft accidents result from a single cause - 10.48.8

Question 5:

We can control the odds in aviation by training and avoiding high-risk situations. Setting personal limitations and decision points and utilizing processes such as the DECIDE model have the following benefits:

These models have no benefit in aviation as they are designed for ground-based transport
These types of models are useful because they aid the decision making process
These models are only useful for large jet operations
These types of models make the operation more efficient so save money
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: These types of models are useful because they aid the decision making process - 10.48.18

Question 6:

The ability to make reasonable judgements and therefore good decisions comes from?

The CAA rules
Rues and regulations and primarily from standard operating procedures
Having good situational awareness and adequate residual mental capacity
The aircraft manufacturers documents and checklists
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Having good situational awareness and adequate residual mental capacity - 10.48.12

Question 7:

We use information obtained from our short-term memory to make a decision. Which factors below can affect your decision making process?

The risk of being caught versus the consequences of being caught
What we stand to gain versus what we stand to lose
What are the odds
If the decision is stressed
All of the above
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: All of the above - 10.48.16

Question 8:

Emirates used a decision making model in the 90s that used the menemomic or acronym SADIE. The E in this model is short for?

Estimate the effects
Evaluate the outcome
Enter the report details
Exit the situation
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Evaluate the outcome - 10.48.14

Question 9:

The decision making processes of a pilot during a landing is most probably:

Rule based
Knowledge based
A mixture of rule and Knowledge based
Skill based
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Skill based

Question 10:

British Airways use a decision making model that uses the menemomic or acronym

 DODAR or sometimes FDODAR. The F in this model is short for?

First assess the situation
Find the facts
Fix the problem
Fly the aeroplane
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Fly the aeroplane - 10.48.14
