SHORT TEST: PPL HF 10.14

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.14
Question 1:

If a diver ascends to the water’s surface and shortly thereafter goes flying in an aircraft, the diver will have an increased chance of getting decompression sickness. Because, even though the increased nitrogen solution in the person’s bloodstream caused by the diving may not have been sufficient to cause decompression sickness after a dive, the further decompression associated with a flight at altitude may be sufficient to bring on an attack.  If possible, don’t fly for at least ________ after the last dive.

12 hours
24 hours
36 hours
48 hours
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: 24 hours - 10.16.8

Question 2:

What effect does an increase in ambient pressure (such as during a descent) have on the body?

A change in ambient pressure has no adverse effect on the body
A change in ambient pressure is the primary cause of hypoxia
Trapped gasses will start to expand possibly causing pain
Trapped gasses may not be able to be equalised, possibly causing pain
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Trapped gasses may not be able to be equalised, possibly causing pain - 10.14.8 . Trapped gases will expand when you climb, not in the descent

Question 3:

Which parts of the body are most commonly affected by a pressure differential between them and the outside air pressure, causing pain if that pressure is not equalised? Choose the most correct answer

Outer ears and stomach
Sinuses and joints
Middle ear, sinuses, stomach and cavities in the teeth
Joints and stomach
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Middle ear, sinuses, stomach and cavities in the teeth - 10.14.8

Question 4:

Which parts of the body are considered susceptible to Barotrauma?

Ears
Stomach
Teeth
All of the above
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: All of the above - 10.14.8

Question 5:

If a diver correctly ascends to the water’s surface and shortly thereafter goes flying in an aircraft, the diver will have an increased chance of getting decompression sickness. Because, even though the increased nitrogen solution in the person’s bloodstream caused by the diving may not have been sufficient to cause decompression sickness after a dive, the further decompression associated with a flight at altitude may be sufficient to bring on an attack.

True
False
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: True - 10.16.12

Question 6:

What is the MAIN cause of barotrauma?

A gradual change in air pressure that affects enclosed cavities within the body such as the middle ear
A sudden change in air pressure that affects enclosed cavities within the body such as the middle ear
A deficiency in the amount of oxygen reaching bodily tissues
Flying within 24 Hrs of going diving
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: A sudden change in air pressure that affects enclosed cavities within the body such as the middle ear - 10.14.4

Question 7:

A dangerous and occasionally lethal condition caused by nitrogen bubbles that form in the blood and other tissues of scuba divers who surface too quickly is known as?

Decompression sickness
Hypoxia
Hyperventilation
Oxygen paradox
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Decompression sickness - 10.16.2

Question 8:

How much can trapped gasses in the body expand at an altitude of 7,000ft?

10%
40%
100%
140%
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: 40% - 10.14.4

Question 9:

One of the MAIN symptoms that indicate a person is suffering from Barotrauma is?

Tingling of the hands, legs and feet
Pain in the ears or stomach
A decreased breathing rate
A feeling of euphoria and general well-being
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Pain in the ears or stomach - 10.14.6 , Option one is incorrect because Tingling of the hands, legs and feet is a symptom of Hyperventilation. Option three is not correct because decreasing the breathing rate overcomes hyperventilation and is not a symptom of Barotrauma. Option 4 is a symptom of Hypoxia not barotrauma.

Question 10:

Several cavities in the body naturally contain gas that can become trapped during a climb, and pain may be experienced. This pain is known as ____________ and affects the stomach, intestines, the sinuses and sometimes teeth.

Decompression sickness
Hypoxia
Barotrauma
Oxygen paradox
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Barotrauma - 10.14.2. The correct answer is Barotrauma because that is associated with all the symptoms. Option one is not correct because decompression sickness is a type of barotrauma that is only associated with joints.

Question 11:

A pilot whom is suffering from a head cold most likely will have a blocked Eustachian tube. It is recommended that you do not go flying with a head cold because this may present a serious hazard when flying due to the potential of which condition?

Decompression sickness
Hypoxia
Barotrauma
Oxygen paradox
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Barotrauma - 10.14.10. Option one is not correct because even though decompression sickness is a type of barotrauma, it is only associated a build up of Nitrogen within the joints and not the Eustachian tube.

Question 12:

In some cases, especially if there is congestion, air may not be able to pass freely through the Eustachian tube.  This condition is known as a barotrauma or "ear block", symptoms of which can range from muffled hearing to excruciating pain.  If left untreated, rupture or perforation of the eardrum can occur. During the ascent, when air must be expelled out of the middle ear, yawning, chewing, or swallowing all may be effective in helping to open the eustachian tube.  On descent (which is generally when most ear blocks occur), when air must be forced back into the middle ear, the above may also be useful, but the definitive "treatment" is to ______________ ?

Place a paper bag over the mouth and try to get the person to regulate their breathing
Perform a Valsalva manoeuvre
Perform a faster descent
Perform a slower descent
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Perform a Valsalva manoeuvre - 10.14.12

Question 13:

Decompression sickness is commonly known as “the bends”. Select the correct statement below:

The bends has the same treatment as hypoxia
If not treated, the bends can lead to unconsciousness and death
The bends is more likely to occur when flying rather than scuba diving
The bends is caused by an increase in ambient pressure forcing nitrogen to form bubbles, which become lodged in different parts of the body
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: If not treated, the bends can lead to unconsciousness and death - 10.16.6

Question 14:

What effect does a decrease in ambient pressure (such as during a climb) have on the body?

A change in ambient pressure has no adverse effect on the body
A change in ambient pressure is the primary cause of hypoxia
A change in ambient pressure is the primary cause of hyperventilation
Trapped gasses will start to expand possibly causing pain
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Trapped gasses will start to expand possibly causing pain - 10.14.8

Question 15:

Often the only symptom of possible decompression sickness is manifested by?

Pain in the ears or stomach
Depression, which ranges from mild to severe and is usually limited to the temple
Pain, which ranges from mild to severe and is usually limited to the joints
Euphoria
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Pain, which ranges from mild to severe and is usually limited to the joints - 10.16.6
