SHORT TEST: SHORT TEST 10.52

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.52
Question 1:

The majority of consequential errors, such as someone doing the wrong thing or doing something the wrong way, can be categorised as:

Poor error management
Poor threat management
Mistakes, violations or slips
Threats or lack of discipline
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Mistakes, violations or slips

Question 2:

True or False. Consider the statement below with regards to threat and error management and a means of avoiding threats.

Threat and error management does not teach a pilot how to technically fly an airplane; instead, it promotes a proactive philosophy and provides techniques for maximizing safety margins despite the complexity of one’s flying environment.

True
False
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: True - 10.52.10

Question 3:

True or False - In the Swiss Cheese model, an organisation's defences against failure are modelled as a series of barriers, represented as slices of the cheese. The holes in the cheese slices represent individual weaknesses in individual parts of the system, and are continually varying in size and position in all slices. The system as a whole produces failures when holes in all of the slices momentarily align, permitting "a trajectory of accident opportunity", so that a hazard passes through holes in all of the defences, leading to an accident.

True
False
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: true - 10.52.8

Question 4:

What does threat and error management (TEM) deal with?

CRM in multi-crew operations
Documenting and understanding errors made in accidents and incidents in civil aviation
Awareness of the potential risks to safe flying and understanding that errors can be eliminated
Awareness of the potential threats to safe flying and mitigating the potential for errors
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Awareness of the potential threats to safe flying and mitigating the potential for errors - 10.52.2 . Errors can not be eliminated (only reduced or mitigated)

Question 5:

What are considered the top three threats that could potentially affect a safe flight?

Flight plan errors, first solo flight and fuel contamination
Planning errors, fuel contamination and aircraft malfunctions
Pilot incapacitation, security issues and avionics failures
ATC issues, aircraft malfunctions and weather issues
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: ATC issues, aircraft malfunctions and weather issues

Question 6:

There are now a wide number of models and methods available for managing human error and performance in aviation. Which of the following models below are popular within aviation?

Crew Resource Management (CRM)
Single Pilot Resource Management (SPRM)
Line Operations Safety Audits (LOSA)
Safety Management Systems (SMS)
Threat and Error Management (TEM)
All of the above
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: all of the above - 10.52.4
