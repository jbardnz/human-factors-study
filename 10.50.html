10.50 Social Psychology and Flight Deck Management



10.50.2 Explain how outside resources, such as ATC, engineers and other pilots can contribute to a pilot’s management of a flight.

The original reason for more than one pilot was redundancy. A second pilot could take over if the first pilot collapsed, became ill, or died at the controls. Later, as aircraft became more complex and traffic denser, it was wise to have the second crew member to help handle the higher workload.

Later still, as safety issues were studied more thoroughly, it became clear that we are all prone to error, and therefore the presence of a second pilot could help to prevent those inevitable errors from getting to the stage where they jeopardised safety.

As a PPL pilot you will not normally have the advantage of a two crew flight deck, but you can use the resources you have when managing the flight.

The concept of crew resource management (CRM). 

Briefly defined, crew resource management (CRM) is the effective use of all available resources, i.e. equipment, procedures and people, to achieve safe and efficient flight operations.
  • Yourself
  • Others (another pilot, engineer, ground crew/company staff/CFI, ATC, passengers)
  • Information (weather briefings, flight manuals, AIP, pilot reports)
  • Fuel/time
  • Aircraft


Crew Resource Management describes the process of managing and using all the people, equipment and information available on the flight deck.

Here are some examples of using all resources to help make decisions:
  • Ask nearby pilots about weather conditions ahead
  • Ask ATC if there has been any pilot reports on your route
  • Listen to airport frequencies well ahead of arrival to assess traffic
  • Call engineers on a cell phone if you need more information on an aircraft defect
  • Ask ATC for help if you are unsure of your position
  • If there is an experienced pilot on board you can ask for their opinion prior to making a decision
  • If there is a specialist medical person you can communicate with, they can give advice during a medical emergency
  • The aircraft flight manual may have useful information for an abnormal situation
  • You may be able to contact an instructor or CFI to help with a difficult decision
