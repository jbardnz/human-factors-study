SHORT TEST: SHORT TEST 10.38

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.38
Question 1:

If stress occurs over a long time and no allowance is made for it, what other psychological effects may manifest? Choose all of the correct answers.

If the stress becomes very acute or severe the pilot may begin to have a break down or begin to act out certain non-typical behaviors. Acting out can include non-characteristic tough guy or macho displays, in an attempt to hide the level of stress within.
If the stress occurs over a long time and no allowance is made for it, chronic fatigue will usually set in
If the stress occurs over a long time and no allowance is made for it , making mistakes and forgetting important tasks becomes common
If the stress occurs over a long time and no allowance is made for it , sweating forehead and/or palms will occur
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Sweating forehead and/or palms are Physiological effects of stress, not a Psychological effect - 10.38.18

Question 2:

A good definition of stress would be?

Being so unaware of your environment you don’t notice things happening
An excess of environmental demands over an individual’s capability to meet them
Suffering from constant headaches
Over stimulation of the cranial cortex
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: An excess of environmental demands over an individual’s capability to meet them - 10.38.2

Question 3:

If mental workload is too high, or too much information is being received from the body sensors, or the level of mental activity is too great, the brain can become ‘overloaded’ and:

You will not be able to solve all of life’s problems at once
Arousal will be too low, so the pilot will begin to neglect some information or tasks, and normal functioning will begin to break down
Arousal will be too high, so the pilot will begin to neglect some information or tasks, and normal functioning will begin to break down
Load Shedding will not automatically occur and problems will most likely follow
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Arousal will be too high, so the pilot will begin to neglect some information or tasks, and normal functioning will begin to break down

Question 4:

The difference between acute and chronic stress and fatigue is best summed up as?

If the stress becomes very acute or severe the pilot may begin to have a break down or begin to act out certain non-typical behaviors. Acting out can include non-characteristic tough guy or macho displays, in an attempt to hide the level of stress within.
If the stress occurs over a long time and no allowance is made for it, chronic fatigue will usually set in.
Acute is short lasting, a common cause of chronic fatigue is insufficient good-quality sleep, perhaps caused through continuous time zone changes or shift work
All of the above are correct
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: All of the above are correct - 10.38.14

Question 5:

Stress has certain physiological effects on a person. Which of the following are physiological effects of stress? Choose all correct answers.

Nervousness or shaking of the hands
Sweating forehead and/or palms
Awareness of the heart beating
Forgetting important tasks (Load shedding)
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer:Nervousness, Sweating, and Awareness of the heart beating - 10.38.16 Load shedding is a sign of stress but not a physiological symptom

Question 6:

Environmental stressors such as vibrations transmitted to the body from the aircraft via the seat, seatbelts and floor can affect pilot performance in which ways?

They can cause dizziness and lead to hypoxia
Vibrations have been found to have a hypnotic effect and can actually improve a pilot’s performance
Vibrations have been found to have a hypnotic effect and can actually worsen a pilot’s performance
They can make you feel uncomfortable and distract your from your main tasks
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: They can make you feel uncomfortable and distract your from your main tasks - 10.38.10

Question 7:

With regards to the relationship between stress and fatigue, select the correct statement below:

A common cause of fatigue is prolonged periods of rest without working too hard
Fatigue is the accumulation of stress. Fatigue can be a major problem for pilots and occurs because of unresolved stress building up over a period
Stress is the accumulation of fatigue. Stress can be a major problem for pilots and occurs because of unresolved fatigue building up over a period
Fatigue is caused by short bursts of stress over a long period of time.
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Fatigue is the accumulation of stress. Fatigue can be a major problem for pilots and occurs because of unresolved stress building up over a period - 10.38.22

Question 8:

Arousal can best be defined as?

The physiological and psychological state of sense organs stimulated to a point of perception
A state when the level of mental activity is too great whereby the brain can become ‘overloaded’
An excess of environmental demands over an individual’s capability to meet them
A state when too much information is being received from the body sensors
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: The physiological and psychological state of sense organs stimulated to a point of perception - 10.38.6 . Option two and four are incorrect because they define a state of high arousal – not arousal. Option three defines stress.

Question 9:

Which of the following is a direct stressor within aviation?

Flying
Relationship problems
Moving house
Financial problems
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Flying - 10.38.2

Question 10:

A simple model of stress would be?

Being so unaware of your environment you don’t notice things happening
An excess of environmental demands over an individual’s capability to meet them
Suffering from constant headaches
The SHEL model
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: An excess of environmental demands over an individual’s capability to meet them - 10.38.4
