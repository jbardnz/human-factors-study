SHORT TEST: SHORT TEST 10.44

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.44
Question 1:

Where can information in the human brain be stored?

In the motor, sensory and short term memories only
In the action, long term and cognitive memories
In the motor memory, rule memory and decision memories
In the short term, long term, sensation and sensory memories
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: In the short term, long term, sensation and sensory memories - 10.44.6

Question 2:

Which way does information flow between long and short-term memory?

It flows from short-term to long-term memory only
It flows from long-term to short-term memory only
It flows from long-term to short-term memory and vice versa
Information does not flow directly between long and short-term memory
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: It flows from long-term to short-term memory and vice versa - 10.44.4

Question 3:

A process whereby sensory information is organised, identified, and interpreted in order to represent and understand the presented information, or the environment is defined as?

Perception
Reading
Information processing
Expectation
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: perception - 10.44.12

Question 4:

Information contained in this type of memory is under permanent storage with a high capacity and a low rate of decay. However, research shows that once information has been stored, it is never lost. But, information can become more difficult to recall with time. This type of memory is known as?

Sensory memory
Long-term memory
Short-term memory
Working memory
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Long-term memory - 10.44.6

Question 5:

The short-term memory holds the events and information received in the recent past (up to 30 seconds). The capacity of the short-term memory is usually limited to ___________ unrelated items of information that is retained until it is over written - usually after about 30 seconds.

2 - 4
7 ± 2
10 ± 2
up to 15
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: 7 ± 2. The capacity can be increased by association of related items (chunking) - 10.44.6 . The SHORT-TERM MEMORY ACTUALLY MAKES THE DECISION. Information received from the senses is compared with relevant information already stored in the long-term memory. Once a decision has been made some of the short-term memory will pass the relevant information into the long-term memory for comparison in future decision making.

Question 6:

The human process of receiving, sorting, comparing, deciding and storing or discarding data is commonly known as?

Information processing
Memory
Decision-making
Sensory memory processing
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Information processing - 10.44.4

Question 7:

The mental capacity and decision making ability of the human brain is such that:

We can learn new skills and once learnt, they become automatic and mistakes are never made
Humans are able to consciously focus on up to three things at once
There is a fixed amount of information and activity the brain can deal with at any one time and this is unable to be increased with practice
The brain can only consciously focus on one activity or task at a time
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: The brain can only consciously focus on one activity or task at a time - 10.44.4

Question 8:

What is the definition of the term “divided attention”?

The ability to switch attention rapidly from one task to another seemingly monitoring more than one activity at once
The inability to focus on tasks for an extended period
When two people working as a team are focusing on different aspects of the same job
The ability to think about multiple tasks at the same time
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: The ability to switch attention rapidly from one task to another seemingly monitoring more than one activity at once - 10.44.4 . The brain can only give conscious attention to one specific thing at a time. The brain is good at rapidly switching from one task to another. It can effectively divide its attention to monitor and carry out several different activities seemingly at the same time.

Question 9:

A popular method for retaining and retrieving information from memory is the use of mnemonics. This method can best be described as?

The use of a mind map written down on a piece of paper
Reading the same paragraph over and over again until it becomes embedded into your log term memory
A tool to help remember facts or a large amount of information. It can be a song, rhyme, acronym, image, or a phrase to help remember a list of facts in a certain order
A children’s nursery rhyme popular in the Victorian era
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: A tool to help remember facts or a large amount of information. It can be a song, rhyme, acronym, image, or a phrase to help remember a list of facts in a certain order - 10.44.10

True
