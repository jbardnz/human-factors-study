SHORT TEST: SHORT TEST 10.62

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.62
Question 1:

Checklists that are designed in the “Read Do and Verify” method where all operations, from first entering a “cold” aircraft to the “After Engine Shutdown” and “Aircraft Secure” items are covered in the _______________ types of checklists.

Day to day procedures
Normal procedures
Abnormal procedures
Emergency procedures
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Normal procedures - 10.62.4

Question 2:

The disciplined use of checklists is recommended:

To ensure standardised routines for all aircraft types
To speed up the routine actions making operations more efficient
For multi crew operations and IFR operations only
As a threat and error management strategy, to reduce the possibilities of memory lapses
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: As a threat and error management strategy, to reduce the possibilities of memory lapses - 10.62.2

Question 3:

The improper use, or the non-use, of a checklist by flight crews is often cited as the probable cause or at least a contributing factor to?

Poor crew resource management
Repetitive behavior
Aircraft accidents
All of the above are correct
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Aircraft accidents - 10.62.10

Question 4:

The process of conducting a checklist occurs during all flight segments and, in particular, prior to the critical segments which are?

Takeoff, approach, landing
Preflight, takeoff, cruise, landing
Preflight, takeoff, cruise, approach, landing, after landing
Checklists are a distraction to flight and are not recommended for private operations
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Takeoff, approach, landing - 10.62.6

Question 5:

Checklists set up to incorporate “Immediate Memory Items” where actions are required from memory. Are termed _____________ types of checklists.?

Day to day procedures
Normal procedures
Abnormal procedures
Emergency procedures
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Emergency procedures - 10.62.4
