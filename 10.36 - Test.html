SHORT TEST: SHORT TEST 10.36

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.36
Question 1:

One of the most reliable methods of detecting carbon monoxide in the cockpit of an aircraft is?

Your sense of smell
A smoke detector
Listening for the sound of the gas entering the cockpit
A carbon monoxide monitor
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: A carbon mnooxide monitor - 10.36.6

Question 2:

What steps would you take if you suspect that carbon monoxide was in the Cockpit of your aircraft?

Turn the cabin heat fully off
Increase the rate of cabin fresh air ventilation to the maximum
Open windows if the flight profile and aircraft’s operating manual permit such an action
Consider using supplemental oxygen if available
All of the above
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: All of the above -10.36.8

Question 3:

Symptoms of carbon monoxide poisoning are:

Hyperventilation, headache and nausea
Confusion, nausea, headache and unconsciousness
Black-out
Confusion then a sudden black out
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Confusion, nausea, headache and unconsciousness - 10.36.2

Question 4:

What is the most common source of carbon monoxide poisoning in general aviation aircraft?

The fuel tank vent
Cracks in the exhaust muffler
Inadequate sealing of the fire wall
Carburetor fuel leaks
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Cracks in the exhaust muffler - 10.36.4

Question 5:

Carbon monoxide poisoning will effectively induce?

Hypoxia
Hypoglycaemia
Hyperventilation
Tunnel vision
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Hypoxia - 10.36.2
