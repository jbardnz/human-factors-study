The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.10
Question 1:

Hypoxia is the result of which of these conditions?

Low carbon dioxide levels in the blood
Low blood pressure
Low body core temperature
Low Oxygen levels in the blood
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Low Oxygen levels in the blood - 10.10.2

Question 2:

The human body can usually cope reasonably well with decreasing oxygen levels until about:

10,000ft
13,000ft
18,000ft
25,000ft
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: 10,000ft - 10.10.8

Question 3:

Hyperventilation is caused by?

Having an excess of carbon dioxide in the blood which causes a change of the bodies acidity
Having low levels of carbon dioxide in the lungs, which causes a change of the bodies acidity
Too much oxygen being blown out of the lungs without having time to be dissolved into the bloodstream
Having too much adrenalin in the blood which results in the heart beating faster
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Having low levels of carbon dioxide in the lungs, which causes a change of the bodies acidity - 10.12.4

Question 4:

Which of the following functions of the eye are NOT immediately affected by hypoxia?

Peripheral vision
Light sensitivity
Sharpness of vision
Colour vision
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Light sensitivity - 10.10.14

Question 5:

Loss of cabin pressure may result in Hypoxia because as cabin altitude increases:

The percentage of Nitrogen in the air is increased
The percentage of Oxygen in the air is decreased
The percentage of Oxygen in the air is increased
Oxygen partial pressure is decreased
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Oxygen partial pressure is decreased - 10.10.6 . Option one is incorrect because the percentage of Nitrogen, carbon dioxide and Oxygen in the atmosphere remains constant with changes in altitude. There is just less pressure as you increase in altitude. Option two and three are incorrect because oxygen remains at about 21% in the atmosphere. i.e. from the ground out to space. The amount of oxygen decreases, however due to lower barometric pressure.

Question 6:

A condition characterized by abnormally prolonged and rapid breathing, resulting in decreased carbon dioxide levels and increased oxygen levels that produce faintness, tingling of the fingers and toes, and, if continued, alkalosis and loss of consciousness. Is called?

Hyperventilation
Oxygen Paradox
Hypoxia
Panic
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Hyperventilation - 10.12.2

Question 7:

What are the main differences between hyperventilation and hypoxia?

Hypoxia is caused by an excess in the amount of oxygen reaching bodily tissues and it is usually treated by descending to a lower altitude and/or administering 100% oxygen. Hyperventilation is characterised by an abnormal increase in the rate and depth of breathing, resulting from abnormally low levels of carbon dioxide in the blood
Hyperventilation is caused by a deficiency in the amount of oxygen reaching bodily tissues and it is usually treated by descending to a lower altitude and/or administering 100% oxygen. Hypoxia is characterised by an abnormal increase in the rate and depth of breathing, resulting from abnormally low levels of carbon dioxide in the blood
Hypoxia is caused by a deficiency in the amount of oxygen reaching bodily tissues and it is usually treated by descending to a lower altitude and/or administering 100% oxygen. Hyperventilation is characterised by an abnormal increase in the rate and depth of breathing, resulting from abnormally low levels of carbon dioxide in the blood
Hyperventilation is caused by a deficiency in the amount of oxygen reaching bodily tissues and it is usually treated by descending to a lower altitude and/or administering 100% oxygen. Hypoxia is characterised by an abnormal decrease in the rate and depth of breathing, resulting from abnormally high levels of carbon dioxide in the blood
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Hypoxia is caused by a deficiency in the amount of oxygen reaching bodily tissues and it is usually treated by descending to a lower altitude and/or administering 100% oxygen. Hyperventilation is characterised by an abnormal increase in the rate and depth of breathing, resulting from abnormally low levels of carbon dioxide in the blood - 10.12.10

Question 8:

Select the correct statement below:

A healthy pilot should not be affected significantly by hypoxia above 10,000ft amsl.
If a pilot is unwell with a chest infection it is not possible to suffer effects of hypoxia below 10,000 ft.
The most complex parts of the brain use the most amount of oxygen so will be affected earliest by hypoxia
Hypoxia will affect all pilots in the same way
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: The most complex parts of the brain use the most amount of oxygen so will be affected earliest by hypoxia - 10.10.18

Question 9:

Which would most likely cause Hyperventilation?

The excessive consumption of alcohol
An extremely slow rate of breathing and insufficient oxygen
A stressful situation causing anxiety
Excessive exercise
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: A stressful situation causing anxiety - 10.12.4 , Hyperventilation results from an abnormal increase in the volume of air breathed in and out of the lungs. It can occur subconsciously when a stressful situation is encountered.

Question 10:

If a pilot becomes hypoxic, there is a certain length of time available to recognise that hypoxia exists and remain conscious enough to be able to do something about it. This period is known as?

The time of useful consciousness
Time of visual awareness
Time of Hypoxic onset
The time of effective consciousness
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: The time of useful consciousness - 10.10.22

Question 11:

An effective way to prevent or treat hypoxia is?

Administering oxygen from an oxygen supply source
Descending the aircraft to 10,000ft AMSL or below
Increasing the aircraft airspeed to improve Ram Air Effect
Both option one and two are correct
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Both option one and two are correct - 10.10.16

Question 12:

Hypoxia affects vision and cognitive performance causing:

An increase in visual sharpness, dimming, tunnel vision and increased colour vision
Loss of visual sharpness, dimming, tunnel vision and reduced colour vision
Increases visual acuity and mental agility
Personality, reasoning, memory, judgement, decision-making changes
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Loss of visual sharpness, dimming, tunnel vision and reduced colour vision - 10.10.14

Question 13:

At sea level, the normal partial pressure of oxygen outside the lungs that is breathed in is ?

104 MM HG, this partial pressure of oxygen outside the lungs reduces once breathed in, due to the presence of water vapour and carbon dioxide
104 MM HG, this partial pressure of oxygen outside the lungs increases once breathed in, due to the presence of water vapour and carbon dioxide
159 MM HG, this partial pressure of oxygen outside the lungs reduces once breathed in, due to the presence of water vapour and carbon dioxide
159 MM HG, this partial pressure of oxygen outside the lungs increases once breathed in, due to the presence of water vapour and carbon dioxide
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: 159 MM HG, this partial pressure of oxygen outside the lungs reduces once breathed in, due to the presence of water vapour and carbon dioxide - 10.10.4 . Option one is incorrect because 102 MM HG is the MINIMUM required partial pressure of oxygen in the lungs for normal human functioning. (102 MMHg is approximately the amount experienced at 10,000 ft AMSL)

Question 14:

Which is a common symptom of Hyperventilation?

Light-headedness, tingling of the hands, legs and feet
Increased vision keenness
Decreased breathing rate
A feeling of euphoria and general wellbeing
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Light-headedness, tingling of the hands, legs and feet - 10.12.6 , . Option two is not correct because hyperventilation distorts ones abilities, not increases them. Option three is not correct because decreasing the breathing rate overcomes hyperventilation and is not a symptom of it. Option 4 is a symptom of Hypoxia not hyperventilation.

15 – 30 minutes
3-5 minutes
30 minutes or more
15-45 Seconds

Question 16:

What is the main reason why hypoxia symptoms are difficult to detect?

A healthy pilot should not be affected significantly by hypoxia above 10,000ft amsl
The most complex parts of the brain use the most amount of oxygen, so they will be affected the latest by hypoxia
The onset of hypoxia can be subtle so, the sufferer may not be aware of it
Hypoxia will affect all pilots in the same way

Incorrect

Question 17:

Hypoxia is the result of which of these conditions?

Insufficient oxygen reaching the brain
Excessive carbon dioxide in the bloodstream
Limited oxygen reaching the heart muscles
Low blood sugar levels
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Insufficient oxygen reaching the brain - 10.10.2 . Hypoxia is a state of oxygen deficiency in the body sufficient to impair functions of the brain and other organs. The second option is incorrect because insufficient, not excessive carbon dioxide in the bloodstream describes hyperventilation
