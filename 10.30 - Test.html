SHORT TEST: SHORT TEST 10.30

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.30
Question 1:

Obesity can lead to a variety of diseases including hypertension, diabetes, and gout - which in turn can lead to coronary heart disease. The extra load on the joints can lead to arthritic problems too.What is considered a healthy weight?

A Body Mass Index (BMI) of below 18.5.
A Body Mass Index (BMI) of between 18.5 and 25.
A Body Mass Index (BMI) of between 25 and 30.
A Body Mass Index (BMI) of between 30 and 35.
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: A Body Mass Index (BMI) of between 18.5 and 25 - 10.30.12

Question 2:

Fitness to fly can best be described as:

Ensuring that you exercise before and after each flight
Being “fit” mentally to withstand the considerable and varying demands and rigors that can arise within the flying environment
Being “fit” physically to withstand the considerable and varying demands and rigors that can arise within the flying environment
Being “fit” both mentally and physically to withstand the considerable and varying demands and rigors that can arise within the flying environment
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Being “fit” both mentally and physically to withstand the considerable and varying demands and rigors that can arise within the flying environment - 10.30.2

Question 3:

The pilot always has to ask the simple question before each and every flight: Am I fit to fly? Remember that any flight has the potential to become very demanding, often without warning. A useful pneumonic to jog the pilots’ memories before each flight is?

IM SAFE
IM SWEET
I AM OK
ALL GOOD
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: IM SAFE - 10.30.8

Question 4:

Who is responsible to ensure that your pilot medical is up to date?

It is the responsibility of the CAA.
It is the responsibility of your Designated Aviation Medical Examiner.
It is the responsibility of your training organisation.
It is the responsibility of each individual pilot to ensure medicals are current.
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: It is the responsibility of each individual pilot to ensure medicals are current. - 10.30.4

Question 5:

20
30

60

Dehydration

Incorrect

Question 7:

Their training organisation
Their aviation medical examiner
Their general practitioner
The CAA

Incorrect
Answer: Their Aviation medical examiner - 10.30.6

Fasting

Feelings

5% 40-60%
10% 20-30%

Incorrect

Question 10:

How can pilots help reduce high blood pressure? Choose all correct options 

Increasing physical fitness.
Altering diet, reducing salt intake.
Reducing alcohol intake
Reducing stress levels.
Reducing weight.

Incorrect

Question 11:

Flying with a heavy cold or blocked sinus, hay fever, influenza etc:

Can cause decompression sickness to occur
Can cause you to think faster and cause anxiety
Can cause sinus pain and damage the middle ear
Can cause flicker vertigo and disorientation
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Can cause sinus pain and damage the middle ear - 10.30.12

Question 12:

Select the correct statement below:

There is no known physiological reason why expectant mothers to be should not fly while pregnant, assuming they and their baby remain in good health
Any significant anaemia, nausea, tiredness or symptoms of low blood pressure (all common during pregnancy) would be compatible with flying as a pilot
It is generally accepted and recommended that pregnant pilots should not fly much beyond four months of pregnancy
It is generally accepted and recommended that pregnant pilots should not fly much beyond the first semester
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: There is no known physiological reason why such expectant mothers to be should not fly while pregnant, assuming they and their baby remain in good health - 10.30.10

Question 13:

With regards to smoking and flying, select the correct statement below?

Smoking improves lung capacity
There is no direct threat to flight safety created by a pilot smoking however, it should be realized that smoking is a leading cause of disease and death throughout the world

Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: There is no direct threat to flight safety created by a pilot smoking however, it should be realized that smoking is a leading cause of disease and death throughout the world - 10.30.12
