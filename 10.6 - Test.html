Question 10:

At sea level the atmospheric air pressure is 760 mm Hg. Because oxygen is about 20 percent of the atmosphere, its presence is responsible for 20 percent of the total sea level pressure.

As your altitude increases, what happens to the partial pressure of the oxygen in the atmosphere?

The partial pressure of Oxygen does not change as altitude increases
As altitude increases the partial pressure of Oxygen also increases
As altitude increases the partial pressure of Oxygen decreases
All of the above are correct
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: As altitude increases the partial pressure of Oxygen decreases. 10.6.8 As the atmospheric pressure decreases with increasing altitude, the partial pressure of oxygen also decreases. The decreased partial pressure makes it harder for the oxygen molecules to cross the alveolar membrane and get into the bloodstream. The percentage of molecules of oxygen in the air does not change.
