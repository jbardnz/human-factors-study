SHORT TEST: SHORT TEST 10.24

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.24
Question 1:

A condition where there is a total loss of vision due to G forces is called ________? It is not the same as unconsciousness although this will eventually result if he G forces are prolonged or further increased. 

Pre unconsciousness
Black out
Red out
Glaucoma
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Black out - 10.24.4

Question 2:

Positive accelerations affect the circulatory system because they tend to?

Create a feeling of euphoria
Cause blood in the pilot’s body to be forced upwards (i.e. it drains away from the stomach and legs towards the brain)
Cause blood in the pilot’s head to be forced downward (i.e. it drains away from the brain and towards the stomach and legs)
Cause a sensation of floating on air
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Cause blood in the pilot’s head to be forced downward (i.e. it drains away from the brain and towards the stomach and legs) - 10.24.2

Question 3:

The pilot’s ability to maintain consciousness is related to?

Being able to maintain blood pressure to the brain
The amount of glucose consumed in the previous 24 hrs
Being able to maintain blood pressure to the abdomen and legs
How much sleep had the night before
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Being able to maintain blood pressure to the brain - 10.24.2

Question 4:

The application of Positive accelerations can result in which effects on a pilot’s vision?

A loss of colour
A loss of peripheral vision
Blurring of vision
Blacking out
All of the above
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: All of the above - 10.24.2

Question 5:

When an aircraft is flying under negative G loading, the pilot can run into blood congestion problems with blood having difficulty flowing back to the heart. This can lead to a condition called  ___________? This is a condition where blood pools in the eyes under pressure. In extreme cases this excessive pressure can lead to glaucoma and blindness.

A loss of peripheral vision
Red out
Blacking out
A loss of colour
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Red out - 10.24.
