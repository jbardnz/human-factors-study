SHORT TEST: SHORT TEST 10.18

The following Short Test is designed to help you review the course material.

Your answers will generate instant feedback - you can redo the question if it is incorrect.

The results from this test will not contribute to your FINAL EXAM score

PPL HF 10.18
Question 1:

What is the most effective way to use the eyes when looking outside during night flight?

Look only at far away, dim lights
Scan slowly to permit off centre viewing
Concentrate directly on each object for a few seconds
Ensure your sunglasses are polarised
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Scan slowly to permit off centre viewing - 10.18.18 . Physiologically, the eyes are most effective at seeing objects off centre at night. Accordingly a pilot should scan slowly to permit off centre viewing. Option one is incorrect because pilots must look at their gauges and instruments, which are about 60cm in front of them. Option three is incorrect because peripheral (off centre) vision is more effective at night

Question 20:

Question 21:

The same distance from the runway as when there is no restriction to visibility
Farther from the runway than it actually is
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: Farther from the runway than it actually is - 10.18.34 The pilot who does not recognise this illusion will fly a shallow approach. Option two is incorrect because bright runway and approach lights or a wider than usual runway, NOT haze can give the illusion of being closer to the runway. Option three is not correct because haze can create the illusion of being a greater distance, not the same, from the runway.

Question 27:

Which observed target aircraft would be of most concern with respect to collision avoidance?

One that appears to be ahead and moving from left to right at high speed
One that appears to be ahead and moving from right to left at high speed
One that appears to be ahead and left to right at slow speed
One that appears to be ahead with no lateral or vertical movement and is increasing in size
Correct!
Incorrect
Answer: One that appears to be ahead with no lateral or vertical movement and is increasing in size - 10.18.32 . Any aircraft that appears to have no relative motion and stays in one scan quadrant is likely to be on a collision course. If the target shows no lateral or vertical motion but increases in size, take evasive action.
